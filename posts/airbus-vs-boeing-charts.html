Airbus vs. Boeing: an experiment in charting

My first attempt at creating data visualisations using just HTML & CSS. These charts depict the share of Boeing versus Airbus aircraft for the world's 10 largest airlines.

10 Dec 2022 · 2 mins · Mark Evans

Inspiration for the first chart came from the recent infographic on VisualCapitalist.com which displays the share of men versus the share of women who smoke (for the world's 50 most populous countries).

Their chart was an image, I wanted to see if I could build a similar chart using HTML & CSS. I'm sure there are charting libraries that would let me build something similar, but I wanted full control of the design, and I also wanted to test my knowledge of CSS.

The result is the first chart shown below. In the chart, the vertical axis is used to show the airline's total fleet size, and the horizontal axis shows the percentage split between Boeing and Airbus. The chart is sorted descending by total fleet size (in-service and stored aircraft only).

If the chart were to be rotated 90°, and the elements shared a common baseline, then it would be a mosaic plot (or a Marimekko chart)

The chart above looks OK, and might be suitable for sharing on social media as an infographic, but, I wouldn't add it into a report, and as a Product Manager I wouldn't include it in any of the analytics products I manage. For that I'd probably stick with the ubiquitous stacked column chart.

It's not as visually interesting, but it is more informative (e.g. it's easier to gauge the relative differences between the total fleet sizes). It's still not quite what I'd add to a report (or in one of my products). To get to that point, I'd replace the logos with names, and maybe rotate it through 90° (so it becomes a bar chart), this would let me use horizontal labels for the airlines' names.
